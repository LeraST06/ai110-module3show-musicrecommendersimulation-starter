# Profile Comparison Reflections

## High-Energy Pop vs. Chill Lofi
These two profiles returned almost completely different songs, which makes sense. The pop profile wanted loud and upbeat; the lofi profile wanted quiet and acoustic. The acousticness bonus helped push the right songs to the top for the lofi user. The system separated these two listener types well.

## Deep Intense Rock vs. Classical Fan Wanting High Energy
Both wanted high energy but the results were very different. Rock got Storm Runner, which was a perfect match. Classical got a slow, quiet piano piece at the top because it was the only classical song in the catalog and the genre bonus was enough to win. High energy was completely ignored in that case.

## High-Energy Pop vs. Deep Intense Rock
Both profiles are high energy but different genres and moods. Gym Hero showed up near the top for both, which is a bit odd. For pop users it matched on genre, for rock users it matched on mood ("intense"). The same song serving two very different profiles is a sign the catalog is too small to give truly personalized results.

## Conflicting Sad + High Energy vs. Chill Lofi
The conflicting profile returned a scattered, inconsistent list. A quiet folk song ranked second just because it matched the "sad" mood, even though the user wanted high energy. The lofi profile, by contrast, returned a tight and consistent list because all its preferences pointed in the same direction. When preferences contradict each other, the scoring just adds up points without noticing the conflict.
